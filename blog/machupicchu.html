The Sacred Valley of Peru

The Takeaway

The journey to Machu Picchu changed me. The mystery and utter strangeness of the place rekindled a sense of wonder and awe that I had lost somewhere. The city itself is simply an impossibility, perched atop steep cliffs, banked on 3 sides by a massive gorge and a rushing river, beyond which are mountains beyond mountains as far as the eye can see, all covered in a green blanket of cloud forest and jungle. So save up and go. If anything, go to see the majesty of the place itself and get in some spectacular cloud-forest hiking.

A quick trip to the Sacred Valley, including Machu Picchu will reveal all sorts of majestic landscapes, ruins, wonders, and marvels (not to mention great food and culture). But to really understand the amazement and mystery of the place, you need to do your research. A good place to start is the fantastic travel/history/adventure book, Turn Right at Machu Picchu: Rediscovering the Lost City One Step at a Time by Mark Adams. Also, The Last Days of the Incas by Kim MacQuarrie depicts the tragedy of war and conquest between the Inca and the Spanish colonists.

For me, reading and researching before the trip, asking lots of questions, and visiting the museums in Cuzco during our trip made the mystery that much more tangible, as ancient ruins are still being uncovered from the jungles of Peru to this day, and the presence of the great Inca ruins begs the question, "How did they do it?" To this day, we don't have a good answer.

Finally, to add mystery upon mystery, and tragedy upon tragedy, the late rapper, 2Pac was named after the last Inca king that made a valiant last stand against the Spanish and ultimately paid with his life, Tupac Amaru.
